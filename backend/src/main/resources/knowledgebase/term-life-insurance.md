# Term Life Insurance

## Overview
Term Life Insurance provides coverage for a specific period (term), typically 10, 20, or 30 years. It offers affordable protection with a fixed premium during the term.

## Key Features
- **Fixed Premium**: Premiums remain constant throughout the term
- **Temporary Coverage**: Coverage ends when the term expires
- **Affordable**: Lower premiums compared to permanent life insurance
- **Death Benefit**: Pays a tax-free death benefit to beneficiaries if the insured dies during the term
- **No Cash Value**: Unlike whole life, term insurance does not accumulate cash value

## Benefits
- **Financial Protection**: Provides financial security for dependents
- **Budget-Friendly**: Most affordable life insurance option
- **Flexibility**: Can be converted to permanent insurance in many cases
- **Coverage Amount**: Can choose coverage amounts based on needs
- **Simple**: Straightforward policy with clear terms

## Suitable For
- Young families with children
- Homeowners with mortgages
- People with temporary financial obligations
- Those seeking maximum coverage for minimum cost
- Individuals who need coverage until retirement

## Premium Structure
- Premiums are based on age, health, coverage amount, and term length
- Younger, healthier individuals pay lower premiums
- Premiums increase significantly if policy is renewed after term expires

## Eligibility
- Age: Typically 18-75 years old
- Health: Medical exam may be required for larger coverage amounts
- Income: Must demonstrate ability to pay premiums

## Common Questions

**Q: What happens when the term ends?**
A: Coverage ends. You can renew (at higher rates), convert to permanent insurance, or let it expire.

**Q: Can I cancel my policy?**
A: Yes, you can cancel at any time, but you won't receive any refund of premiums paid.

**Q: Is term life insurance worth it?**
A: Yes, if you need affordable coverage for a specific period, such as until children are grown or mortgage is paid.

**Q: What if I outlive the term?**
A: The policy expires with no payout. This is why term insurance is more affordable than permanent insurance.

## Comparison with Other Products
- **vs Whole Life**: Lower premiums, no cash value, temporary coverage
- **vs Universal Life**: Simpler, no investment component, fixed term
- **Best for**: Those who need maximum coverage for a specific period at the lowest cost
