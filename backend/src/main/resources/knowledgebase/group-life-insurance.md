# Group Life Insurance

## Overview
Group Life Insurance is life insurance coverage provided by an employer or organization to a group of people, typically employees. It offers affordable coverage as part of employee benefits packages.

## Key Features
- **Employer-Sponsored**: Usually provided through employer
- **Affordable**: Lower premiums due to group rates
- **Easy Enrollment**: Often no medical exam required
- **Basic Coverage**: Typically provides 1-2x annual salary
- **Portability**: May be able to convert to individual policy if leaving employer

## Benefits
- **Low Cost**: Group rates make it very affordable
- **No Medical Exam**: Often available without health questions
- **Convenience**: Easy enrollment through employer
- **Immediate Coverage**: Coverage often starts immediately
- **Additional Options**: May offer supplemental coverage options

## Suitable For
- Employees offered through employer
- Those who want basic, affordable coverage
- People who may not qualify for individual insurance
- Those seeking supplemental coverage
- Employees who want to supplement individual policies

## Coverage Types
- **Basic Coverage**: Usually 1x annual salary, often employer-paid
- **Supplemental Coverage**: Additional coverage employee can purchase
- **Dependent Coverage**: May include coverage for spouse/children
- **Voluntary Coverage**: Employee-paid additional coverage

## Premium Structure
- Often employer pays for basic coverage
- Employee pays for supplemental coverage
- Premiums deducted from paycheck
- Group rates are typically lower than individual rates
- Premiums may increase with age

## Portability
- Coverage typically ends when employment ends
- May have option to convert to individual policy
- Conversion must usually be done within 30 days of leaving
- Converted policy may have higher premiums
- Some policies offer portability without conversion

## Limitations
- **Limited Coverage**: Usually lower coverage amounts than individual policies
- **Employment Dependent**: Coverage ends when you leave employer
- **Less Customization**: Fewer options than individual policies
- **Rate Changes**: Premiums may increase with age or at renewal

## Eligibility
- Must be employee of participating organization
- Usually available to all employees (no health questions for basic coverage)
- May have waiting period (30-90 days)
- Part-time employees may have limited eligibility

## Common Questions

**Q: What happens if I leave my job?**
A: Coverage typically ends. You may have option to convert to individual policy within 30 days, or coverage may be portable.

**Q: Is group life insurance enough?**
A: It provides basic coverage but may not be sufficient for all needs. Many people supplement with individual policies.

**Q: Can I keep my group policy?**
A: Usually no, unless you convert to individual policy or have portability option. Check your specific policy terms.

**Q: How much does it cost?**
A: Basic coverage is often free (employer-paid). Supplemental coverage is typically very affordable due to group rates.

## Comparison with Other Products
- **vs Individual Term Life**: Lower cost, less coverage, employment-dependent, easier to qualify
- **vs Whole Life**: Much lower cost, temporary coverage, no cash value, employer-dependent
- **Best for**: Employees who want affordable basic coverage or supplemental coverage through employer
